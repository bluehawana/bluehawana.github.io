Attended the “Microsoft Agentic AI Hack” today at World of Volvo and finished the task first. 

What I learned is that regardless of how advanced the agent frameworks become, infrastructure remains crucial for the success of your project. Much of the time was spent on typical developer tasks such as:

- SSL/proxy issues
- Python version conflicts
- Data seeding
- Azure login/logout loops
- Agent registration mismatches
- Installing uv/Aspire/truststores

Essentially, everything except “AI.” I didn’t have enough time to fully grasp what each agent was designed to solve. Personally, I still prefer running things on-prem on my Mac. Once you commit to one ecosystem (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google), you lose the flexibility to choose.

Currently, I’m running Claude Code with Ollama Qwen 3.6 locally on an M5 Max MacBook Pro. I replicated the entire hackathon setup at home in about 30 minutes—clean and simple.

AI is evolving rapidly, but the fundamentals remain the same: computing, networking, environment setup, and debugging. If you can’t manage those, the agents won’t be of much help.
