New Year, New Project: No.8 — KB-Graph: A Self-Learning Math Island for My Daughter

What if studying gymnasium math felt like playing Animal Crossing instead of grinding a textbook? This idea sparked Project No.8. This August, my daughter starts spetsutbildning i matematik at Hvitfeldtska — the accelerated track. In such programs, one hidden knowledge gap can make everything above it collapse.

I discovered "The Math Academy Way" — a 510-page handbook by Justin Skycak (#MathAcademy). It presents knowledge graphs, mastery learning, and spaced repetition — decades of learning science distilled into one openly published draft. While their product is US-focused, the ideas are universal.

Most students never experience learning built on this science. They receive chapters and homework, making studying feel like grinding, leading to less engagement, growing gaps, and ultimately, too late to catch up by exam time. It's time to change the game.

Introducing KB-Graph — a knowledge-graph RAG that transforms the entire Swedish gymnasium curriculum (Gy25) into a self-learning, self-evaluating, self-improving island for one very important user: my daughter.

What it includes:
- 253 topics mapped as a knowledge graph (math åk7 → Matematik 5/spets + Fysik 1–2), aligned with Skolverket’s ämnesplaner
- 454 prerequisite edges + 312 “encompassing” edges — advanced practice silently refreshes fundamentals
- 2,500+ questions generated by a local LLM (Qwen via MLX, zero API cost)
- Every answer verified by SymPy — never trust an LLM with arithmetic
- Self-learning: the graph always knows her frontier — exactly what she’s ready for
- Self-evaluating: instant feedback, accuracy tracking, spaced repetition tuned to her forgetting curve
- Self-improving: struggling topics trigger refinement and targeted remediation

And the Animal Crossing heart: every mastered topic becomes a species in her museum. Algebra is butterflies. Calculus is dinosaurs. Reviews are a daily safari where knowledge fades if not revisited. Rewards come only from demonstrated mastery — no loopholes. Kids find loopholes.

Built entirely with Claude Fable 5 orchestrating the pipeline — digesting the handbook, designing the graph, writing the code, deploying to my VPS, and E2E-testing every flow in three days. AI helping build an AI teacher.

Open knowledge about how humans learn, turned into a private island for one kid. That’s the internet at its best. Her three-year goal sits at the top of her map: Matematik- och fysikprovet → Chalmers. Now she climbs it one confetti at a time.

The site stays private — it’s her island. But if you’re curious about the architecture, the graph schema, or building this for your own kid’s curriculum, comments and DMs are open.
