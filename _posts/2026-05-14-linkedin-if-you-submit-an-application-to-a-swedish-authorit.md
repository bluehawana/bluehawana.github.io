If you submit an application to a Swedish authority, follow every rule, pay every fee, and wait in the queue assigned you, should the rules be allowed to change while you wait?

Lagrådet said no. The government's own inquiry said no. 379 engineers and researchers at companies including Spotify, Klarna, Volvo, Ericsson, and Nvidia said no in their formal remissvar.

The reform itself can move forward on June 6. That isn't what we're contesting. We're asking for one thing: that the roughly 100,000 applications already sitting at Migrationsverket be assessed under the rules that existed when they were filed.

That's what transitional provisions are for. Every Nordic country uses them. Sweden has used them before. The institutions Sweden built to advise on exactly this kind of question recommended them here too.
